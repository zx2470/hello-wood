Protected Areas Yapacana National Park Illegal Mining Pollution

Illegal mining, especially for minerals like gold, can have severe environmental consequences. It often involves the use of harmful chemicals such as mercury and cyanide, deforestation, and the disruption of ecosystems. The pollution resulting from illegal mining can contaminate water sources, harm aquatic life, and affect the health of both the environment and nearby communities.
Protected Areas Canaima National Park Endangered Jaguar

The jaguar (Panthera onca) is the largest big cat native to the Americas and is known for its powerful build and distinctive spotted coat. Jaguars face various threats, including habitat loss, poaching, and conflicts with humans. Their conservation status varies across different regions, but overall, jaguars are listed as "Near Threatened" on the International Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN) Red List. Canaima National Park, located in Venezuela, is a vast and ecologically diverse area that is home to a variety of wildlife, including jaguars. Conservation efforts in the park are crucial to protecting the biodiversity of the region.
